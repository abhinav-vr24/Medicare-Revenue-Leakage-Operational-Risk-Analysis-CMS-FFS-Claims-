Methodology & Data Quality

üìã Dataset Overview

  • Source: CMS Medicare Claims Synthetic Public Use Files (SynPUF)
  • Scope: Carrier (physician/supplier) claims from 2022
  • Volume: 174,411 claim lines after quality filters
  • Coverage: Complete financial chain (submitted ‚Üí allowed ‚Üí paid)
  • Data Quality: 99.86% passed financial logic validation, 0.13% excluded

üî¨ Analysis Framework

Revenue Realization Metrics

  • Realization Rate: (Paid Amount / Allowed Amount) √ó 100
  • Underpayment: Allowed Amount - Paid Amount
  • Collection Efficiency: Provider Payment / Allowed Amount
  • Denial Rate: 1 - Realization Rate

Payment Classifications

  • Zero-Paid: Payment = $0 (denial proxy)
  • Partial-Paid: $0 < Payment < Allowed (adjustment/reduction)
  • Fully-Paid: Payment ‚âà Allowed (normal processing)

Risk Identification

  • Pareto Analysis: 80/20 rule to identify top leakage drivers
  • Outlier Detection: IQR method on underpayment and delay metrics
  • Variation Analysis: Coefficient of variation for process stability

‚öôÔ∏è Technical Implementation

  • Tools: Python (pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn)
  • Data Processing: Multi-stage cleaning with logical consistency checks
  • Validation: Financial logic, date consistency, duplicate detection
  • Segmentation: HCPCS-level, specialty-level, payment status analysis
  • Statistical Methods: Descriptive stats, quartile analysis, outlier flagging

‚ö†Ô∏è Known Limitations

  • Synthetic Data: CMS SynPUF is de-identified and may not reflect all real-world patterns
  • AR Aging: All claims processed within 8 days (Medicare efficiency) ‚Äì traditional 30-90+ day buckets not applicable
  • Denial Codes: Limited explicit denial reason codes; used zero-paid as proxy
  • Payer Mix: Medicare only; commercial payer patterns may differ significantly
  • Context: No SLA or client-specific targets available for benchmarking

Mitigation: Methodology is fully scalable to real-world data with actual denial codes, diverse payer mix, and client-specific KPIs.
